The NIE number in Spain: what it is and how to get it, step by step

By the Vía Sol team•Updated June 2026•7 min read

It's almost always the first question a buyer from the EU asks us: "do I need some special number or permit to buy in Spain?" The short answer is: yes, you need the NIE — and no, it isn't complicated. Here's what it is, how to get it, and how to sidestep the two or three traps that cost people weeks.

What exactly is the NIE?

NIE stands for Número de Identidad de Extranjero — a foreigner's identity number. It's a personal, permanent number that identifies you to the Spanish administration for anything with financial weight: buying a home, signing at the notary, paying taxes, opening a bank account or connecting the electricity. Without it, you simply cannot sign a title deed (escritura).

One common confusion is worth clearing up: the NIE is a number, not a card. How you physically receive it depends on your situation — and this is where being an EU citizen works in your favour.

The good news: you're an EU citizen

As a citizen of the European Union you have freedom of movement and establishment. You need no visa and no residence permit to buy, live or work in Spain. Non-EU citizens receive a physical card (the TIE); you, on the other hand, will never have a TIE. You have two simpler routes:

  • The "plain" NIE (form EX-15): if you're buying a second home or won't yet be living here more than three months. You receive an A4 resolution on paper with your number.
  • The EU-citizen registration certificate (form EX-18): if you'll be resident for more than three months. You receive the famous "green certificate" (an A4 paper and, in many provinces, a small green card) which contains your NIE.

To buy, the NIE alone is enough

The green certificate is a later step, for when you actually decide to settle. For the purchase itself, the number is enough.

Which one do you need?

  • An investment or holiday home → plain NIE (EX-15).
  • A real move → green certificate (EX-18). To get it you'll need to show one of three things: that you work (employed or self-employed), or that you have sufficient funds and health insurance, or that you're a student with insurance. The rough threshold for "sufficient funds" is around the IPREM (about €600 a month per person in 2025).

What documents you need

  • The completed EX-15 (or EX-18) form.
  • A valid passport or national ID, with a copy.
  • Proof of the reason: for example, the reservation or arras contract for the property.
  • The Modelo 790 código 012 fee paid at the bank. It's cheap: around €9.84 for the NIE and €7.31 for the certificate. The exact amount is calculated by the official form itself — go with that figure.

Where and how to apply

  • In Spain: at an Immigration Office (Oficina de Extranjería) or a designated National Police station, always by appointment (cita previa), requested on the administration's online portal. The green certificate is usually issued the same day if your documents are complete.
  • From your home country: at the Spanish consulate. Note: the consulate can only issue the plain NIE; the green residence certificate is obtained in Spain.

The real bottleneck is the appointment

It isn't the paperwork — it's getting a slot. In high-demand areas like the Costa Blanca, appointments vanish fast. Book several weeks ahead… or let us handle it.

Can I get it without setting foot in Spain?

Yes. With a power of attorney (poder notarial) you can authorise a lawyer or gestor to obtain your NIE on your behalf. That power is signed before a notary in Spain, before a Spanish consul abroad, or before a local notary with the Hague Apostille and a sworn translation. It's exactly what many buyers do to have the NIE ready before signing — without travelling twice.

In short

  • The NIE is mandatory to buy; it's a number, not a card.
  • As an EU citizen you need no visa and no TIE: you use the EX-15 (NIE) or EX-18 (green certificate).
  • The fee is a few euros; the hard part is getting the appointment.
  • With a power of attorney, we handle it for you — no travel needed.
